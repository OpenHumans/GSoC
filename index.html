Outreachy

Outreachy is an internship program for open-source projects, for groups traditionally underrepresented in tech. You can read more about Outreachy here: https://www.outreachy.org/

Outreachy Summer 2018

The Outreachy Summer 2018 season is ON! Open Humans is accepted as an Outreachy community and seeks to support three internships. Interested mentors and prospective interns should join #outreachy on our Slack. Please announce yourself, so we know who you are! Why apply?

One of the most important features of the program is that interns are paired with mentors, who are typically experienced developers from Open Humans. The mentor guides the interns to work productively within the community, and helps the interns avoid obstacles and pitfalls. The program is global - interns and mentors may be located anywhere where they have an internet connection (except for countries affected by US trade restrictions), and no travel is required. Thus, aside from the stipend and mentorship aspects, the interns's experience in the internship closely mirrors normal work on distributed development projects. Effective work habits for distributed development are typically not taught as part of computer science curricula, yet are highly desired in the increasingly global and distributed software, IT, and biotechnology industries.

From the viewpoint of Open Humans, the program not only offers to pay interns for contributing, but more importantly, offers an opportunity to recruit new developers who will hopefully go on to become regular, sustaining contributors.

Intern Progress Reports

In addition to writing code, accepted interns participate in the virtual, weekly all-hands community meeting to report on their project's progress. These updates allow us to keep aware of how Outreachy interns are doing, give interns a forum to ask any questions, and promote overall community bonding.

In addition to these meetings interns will also have fortnightly virtual meetings with their mentors to discuss progress, problems and strategies for the next weeks. interns are expected to write a progress report for these meetings. These reports should include:

What did you do in the last two weeks? What do you plan to do in the next two weeks? Do you have any unanswered questions, any unsolved problems from the last weeks, interesting observations or anything else you'd like to mention?
